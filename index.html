Researcher in the Speech and Language Group at IBM Research Lab, India. I received my joint Ph.D. in Computer Science and Engineering from IIT Bombay and Monash University, Melbourne.

My current focus is on Large Language Models for Retrieval Augmented Generation (RAG) and text embedding models for effective information retrieval — including co-authoring IBM's Granite Embedding model family.

AUG 2025
🤖 New Model
Granite Embedding R2 Models Released
Co-authored the next-generation IBM Granite Embedding R2 models — featuring an increased context length, improved inference optimizations, and an updated encoder architecture based on ModernBERT, trained on 2T tokens. Released under Apache 2.0 for both commercial and research use.
arXiv:2508.21085 Read Paper →
FEB 2025
🤖 Technical Report
Granite Embedding Models — IBM's First Open Embedding Family
Released the Granite Embedding model family — encoder-based models for dense and sparse retrieval with English and multilingual capabilities. Techniques include retrieval-oriented pretraining, contrastive finetuning, knowledge distillation, and model merging. Outperforms similar-sized public models on IBM retrieval benchmarks.
arXiv:2502.20204 Read Paper →
